Design

What is Design?

Design is a creative activity that involves planning and creating something functional, often new, to solve a specific problem. The goal of design is to add value and make something more useful for its users.

Graphic Design

Graphic design emphasizes communication. The goal is to ensure that everyone interprets the message in the same way.

Typography

Typography relates to the arrangement of text, making it easier to read and more visually appealing.

Elements of Design

  1. Line
    2. Functions:
    • Organizes composition
    • Creates patterns and textures
    • Communicates movement
    • Creates illusions
    • Highlights or emphasizes specific elements
  2. Shape
    3. Types:
    • Geometric: Precise shapes like squares, triangles, circles, etc.
    • Organic: Irregular shapes found in nature
    • These can be combined to create logos, illustrations, decorations, and patterns.
  3. Color
    • Monochromatic: Different shades of the same color
    • Analogous: Colors next to each other on the color wheel
    • Complementary: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel (contrasting)
    • Split-complementary: A color and the two colors next to its complement
    • Triadic: Three colors equally spaced on the color wheel
    • Tetradic: Four colors forming a rectangle or square on the color wheel
  4. Texture

    5. Refers to the surface quality of a design. Texture makes a design look more real and engaging by giving the viewer a sense of how it would feel to touch the design.

Principles of Design

  1. Unity: The elements of a design should work together as a whole
  2. Balance: The visual weight of elements should be distributed evenly, either symmetrically or asymmetrically
  3. Proportion: The size of elements in a design should be harmonious
  4. Emphasis: The focal point of a design (or the highlight)
  5. Rhythm: The repetition of elements in a design that create movement and flow

These principles and elements form the foundation of effective design. When applied correctly, they enhance both functionality and visual appeal.
